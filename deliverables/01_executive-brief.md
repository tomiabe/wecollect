# Executive Brief — WeCollect Website Revamp

## CEO brief (source)

Rebrand WeCollect as **Africa’s native field process and data collection infrastructure**, emphasizing that WeCollect has:

- **The tool and the people**
- Offerings: **Data collection**, **M&E**, **Data validation**, **Field reporting**
- Packaging: **Tool + people** _or_ **tool-only**

## Primary audience (recommendation)

Your assumption (“enterprise, but honestly all”) is directionally right — but the website needs a **primary buyer** to avoid generic messaging.

Recommended primary audience:

- **Enterprise + institutional buyers** who run field operations and care about **data integrity**, **coverage**, **auditability**, and **operational execution**:
  - research firms, enterprises with large field teams, development orgs, NGOs, government programs, impact/ESG measurement teams

Secondary audiences:

- **Operators** (Project Managers / M&E Leads) who need confidence in workflow + supervision
- **Procurement / leadership** who need trust, security, and proof of capability
- **Partners** (field agent networks, data vendors) where relevant

## Conversion goal (recommendation)

For enterprise-led positioning, “get started for free” is usually the wrong primary conversion because it attracts low-intent traffic and forces product-led onboarding you may not be set up to support.

Recommended primary conversion goal:

- **Book a demo / Talk to sales** (high-intent)

Recommended secondary conversion goals:

- **Request a pilot / Request a quote** (procurement-ready)
- **Download capability deck** (for buyers who need internal alignment)
- **Contact** (fallback)

Optional product-led support (only if the workflow truly exists end-to-end):

- “Try the tool” should be a **secondary** CTA and should not compete with Demo.

## Success metrics (what “better” means)

Marketing/site metrics:

- Demo requests per month (and their qualification rate)
- Capability deck downloads
- % of traffic reaching “Solutions / Use cases / Proof” pages

Sales enablement metrics:

- Shorter time-to-trust in intro calls (measured qualitatively)
- Higher conversion from first call → second call / pilot

## Core promise (from your feature PDF)

The feature PDF already contains the strongest narrative anchors:

- **Offline-first** (works anywhere, syncs when back online)
- **AI-powered from field to report**
- **Trusted data you can prove is clean** (validation/audit layers)

The website should be rebuilt to make these the “spine” of the story and to clearly express:

- What WeCollect is
- Who it is for
- What you offer (the 4 offerings)
- What makes it credible (proof)
- How to buy (tool-only vs tool+people)
