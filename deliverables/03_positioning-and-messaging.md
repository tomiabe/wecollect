# Positioning & Messaging Direction (Recommended)

## Category statement (what WeCollect is)

WeCollect is **Africa’s native field process and data collection infrastructure** — enabling organizations to run field operations end-to-end with **offline capture**, **data validation**, and **reporting**, powered by **tools + a distributed field agent network**.

## Messaging hierarchy (what to say first, second, third)

1) **Promise:** run field operations anywhere in Africa and get data you can trust.
2) **How it works:** offline-first capture + integrity layers + reporting + agent operations.
3) **What you offer (four pillars):**
   - Data collection
   - Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E)
   - Data validation
   - Field reporting
4) **How to buy:** tool-only vs tool + people.
5) **Proof:** coverage, integrity mechanisms, security, and case studies.

## Homepage narrative flow (section blueprint)

1) **Hero**
   - Headline: category statement
   - Subhead: offline + validated + report-ready
   - Primary CTA: Book a demo
   - Secondary CTA: Download capability deck / Request a pilot

2) **Credibility strip**
   - Logos (with short context) + quick metrics (coverage, projects, encryption)

3) **The four offerings**
   - 4 cards: Data collection, M&E, Validation, Field reporting
   - Each: 1 sentence + “Learn more”

4) **Tool + people (packaging)**
   - Two-column comparison:
     - Tool-only: for teams with their own enumerators
     - Tool + people: when you want execution + QA + coverage

5) **Why trust WeCollect (integrity)**
   - Geofencing, rules at capture, auditor loops, truth layers, logs

6) **Use cases**
   - Government & development surveys
   - Consumer & market research
   - Health & humanitarian
   - ESG & impact measurement

7) **Case studies (2–4)**
   - Outcome-led tiles (scale + result), not just logos

8) **How engagement works**
   - Discovery → pilot → rollout → reporting cadence

9) **Final CTA**
   - Book a demo / Request a pilot

## Tone and language (quick rules)

- Prefer “infrastructure”, “integrity”, “auditability”, “coverage”, “operational execution”.
- Avoid “innovative products” and generic SaaS language.
- Replace fluff with specifics: scale, constraints, outcomes, mechanisms.
